Ride Systems
1

Pressure Ride

Full pressure on the ball carrier and all adjacent passing options. Every defender within one pass attacks their mark. High intensity, high turnover potential, but risky if broken.

2

10 Ride

A specifically named player (the "10") applies primary pressure on the ball carrier. Remaining riders position themselves to cut off passing lanes while the 10 forces the action.

3

Contain Ride

No direct pressure on the ball. The ride slows the clear down, maintains shape, and forces the clearing team to use the clock. Low risk, used when protecting a lead.

4

Midline Trap

Allow the clear to develop, then trap the ball carrier as they cross the midfield line. Uses the midfield line as an extra defender. Effective against teams that clear through the middle.

5

Corner Trap

Force the ball carrier toward the sideline or endline corner and trap with a second rider. Uses the boundary as an extra defender. High turnover potential in the corners.

6

Double Ride

Two riders converge on the ball carrier simultaneously. Extremely aggressive -- forces quick decisions. Leaves an open player, so requires excellent off-ball riding by teammates.

7

Zone Ride

Area-based ride coverage. Riders own zones on the field rather than matching up with specific players. Good against structured clearing patterns. Requires communication.

8

Shadow Ride

Match specific riders to specific clearing players based on scouting. The best rider shadows the opponent's best clearing option. Disrupts their primary outlet.

9

Delayed Ride

Let the clear begin without pressure, then apply pressure once the clearing team commits to a pattern. Baits the clear into a predictable shape before attacking it.

10

Turnover Ride

Immediate full-field pressure applied right after a turnover. Capitalizes on the opponent being out of position. Sprint to nearest player and deny the clear before it organizes.

Clear Systems
1

Break Clear

Fast clear up the field immediately after a save or change of possession. Goalie outlets quickly to a defender who pushes the ball up field before the ride sets up. Speed over structure.

2

Wheel Clear

Ball rotates through the defense in a circular pattern. Defenders pass to the next defender in a wheel motion, stretching the ride and finding gaps. Structured and reliable.

3

Reverse Clear

Clear switches field direction against ride pressure. When the ride overloads one side, the clearing team reverses the ball to exploit the weak side. Requires quick, accurate passing.

4

Goalie Carry

Goalie carries the ball to midfield personally. Effective when the ride drops off the goalie. Creates a numerical advantage as the goalie becomes an extra ball carrier.

5

Two-Pass Clear

Quick two-pass clear designed to get through the ride with minimal touches. Goalie to outlet, outlet to midfielder streaking up field. Simple and fast.

6

Midfield Break

A midfielder drops back toward the defensive end to receive the clear pass. Creates an extra option in the defensive third and uses the midfielder's speed to push the ball forward.

7

Corner Clear

Use the corners of the field to escape pressure. Defenders spread to the corners, creating wide passing lanes. The boundary provides protection from rides coming from the middle.

8

Spread Clear

Spread formation across the entire width and depth of the field. Creates maximum spacing and forces the ride to cover a lot of ground. Find gaps in the ride coverage.

9

Quick Clear

Immediate clear after a save -- the goalie outlets before the ride even sets up. Requires pre-planned positions and instant recognition from defenders. Capitalizes on transition chaos.

10

Slow Control

Possession-style clear. No rush to get the ball up field. Move the ball deliberately, wait for the ride to break down, and find the open pass. Used against aggressive rides.

Draw Techniques

Clamp

Push your stick down over the ball on the whistle. The most common draw technique. Goal is to trap the ball and gain immediate control. Requires timing and wrist strength.

Rake

Pull the ball backward toward your own side on the draw. Effective when you want to direct the ball to a wing player behind you. Requires a quick backward motion after the whistle.

Push

Send the ball forward past the opponent on the draw. Directs the ball toward your offensive wing players. Good when your wings are faster and positioned ahead of the opponent's.

Self Control

Center wins and possesses the ball in one motion. The draw taker controls the ball to themselves rather than directing it to a wing. Requires elite stick skills and body positioning.

Directional Draw

Pre-planned draw direction based on scouting and wing strengths. The center aims the ball toward the wing with the best chance of possession. Uses hand signals or verbal codes.

Strategy Codes
P

Push

Direct the draw forward. Center pushes ball ahead toward offensive wings. Used when your attack-side wings are faster or better positioned.

B

Back / Pull

Rake the draw backward toward your defensive wings. Used when you have an advantage on the defensive side of the circle or want to play it safe.

S

Self

Center controls the draw to herself. No directional play -- the center wins and possesses. Called when your center has a clear skill advantage.

L

Left

Direct the draw to the left wing. Specific directional call when the left wing has a positioning or matchup advantage.

R

Right

Direct the draw to the right wing. Specific directional call when the right wing has a positioning or matchup advantage.

Teaching Progression
  • Stage 1 -- Grip & Stance: Teach proper hand placement, body positioning, and foot alignment at the center circle before any live draws.
  • Stage 2 -- Timing: Practice reacting to the whistle. The draw is won or lost in the first tenth of a second. Repetition builds muscle memory.
  • Stage 3 -- Technique: Introduce clamp, rake, and push techniques individually. Master one before adding the next.
  • Stage 4 -- Directional Control: Once technique is solid, practice directing draws to specific wings. Add hand signals and verbal codes.
  • Stage 5 -- Live Reps: Full speed draws with wing players. Practice ground ball recovery, body positioning, and transition after the draw.
  • Stage 6 -- Scouting & Adjustments: Study opponent tendencies. Adjust technique and direction based on the opposing center's habits.
Practice 1: Fundamentals Focus
Total Time: 85 minutes
10 min Warm-Up -- Dynamic stretching, jogging, ladder footwork
15 min Stickwork -- Partner passing (right/left hand), wall ball, catching on the move
10 min Ground Balls -- 1v1 ground ball drills, boxing out, scooping through
15 min 4v3 Drill -- Offensive advantage drill, fast ball movement, finish at goal
20 min Defense Install -- Adjacent slide walkthrough, live 6v6 with coaching stops
15 min Scrimmage -- Full field controlled scrimmage with coaching points
Practice 2: Offensive Install
Total Time: 90 minutes
10 min Warm-Up -- Dynamic stretching, ball handling, high knees with cradling
10 min Stickwork -- Quick stick passing, one-touch catches, off-hand work
25 min Offense Install -- 3-2-2 Motion walkthrough, triangle passing, dodge and drift
15 min 2-Player Games -- Pick and roll, give and go, pick and slip progressions
20 min Scrimmage -- Offensive focus scrimmage, must run 3 passes before shooting
10 min Conditioning -- Sprints, shuttle runs, cool down stretch
Practice 3: Defensive Focus
Total Time: 90 minutes
10 min Warm-Up -- Dynamic stretching, defensive stance slides, reaction drills
15 min Footwork Drills -- Defensive footwork ladders, drop steps, hip turns, closing out
20 min Slide Recognition -- 3v2 / 4v3 slide drill, communication emphasis, adjacent slide reps
15 min Defensive Rotations -- Full 7v7 rotation walkthrough, recovery drills, crease defense
20 min 6v6 Live -- Live play with defensive coaching stops, rewarding communication
10 min Film Review -- Quick video review of defensive principles on tablet/screen
Practice 4: Draw & Transition
Total Time: 90 minutes
10 min Warm-Up -- Dynamic stretching, sprint work, reaction starts
20 min Draw Drills -- Clamp/rake/push technique, wing positioning, live draw reps
15 min Fast Break Drill -- 3v2 and 4v3 transition, outlet passing, finishing in transition
15 min Ride / Clear -- Pressure ride vs. break clear reps, clearing under pressure
20 min Transition Scrimmage -- Draw to goal scrimmage, every possession starts with a draw
10 min Cool Down -- Light jog, static stretching, team huddle
Practice 5: Game Prep
Total Time: 90 minutes
10 min Warm-Up -- Dynamic stretching, game-speed movements, pre-game routine
10 min Stickwork -- Game-speed passing, shooting on the move, quick release shots
15 min Offensive Review -- Walk through primary offense, review reads and triggers
15 min Defensive Review -- Walk through defensive packages, review slide assignments
15 min Special Units -- Draw unit reps, ride/clear reps, EMO/woman-down situations
20 min Controlled Scrimmage -- Game-like scrimmage with officials' signals and sub patterns
5 min Walk Through -- Final walk through of game plan, Q&A, team break
